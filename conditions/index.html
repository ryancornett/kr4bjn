HF Band Conditions

  • A higher Solar Flux Index (SFI) means more radio “fuel” from the Sun, better chances of long-range HF communications.
  • More sunspots (SN) generally mean higher solar activity, which enhances the ionosphere's reflecting power.
  • Geomagnetic indices (K-index, A-index): These measure how calm or disturbed Earth's magnetic field is. Lower values = better for radio propagation; higher values = storms/disruptions.
  • X-ray flux, 304 Å (angstrom) UV radiation, particle flux (protons/electrons), etc.: These indicate more subtle or advanced effects (like absorption in the lower ionosphere, or auroral disturbances). If any of these climb, propagation may degrade.
  • MUF stands for Maximum Usable Frequency — the highest radio frequency at which a short-wave (HF) radio signal can still be reflected by the ionosphere and make long-distance (“skip”) contacts.
  • In simple terms: Above the MUF value, the signal goes through the ionosphere into space, rather than being bounced back to Earth.
  • MUF is path- and time-dependent; what works between two locations at one hour may not work at another.
